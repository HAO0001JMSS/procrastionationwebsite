Conclusion


What did the research reveal?

The research revealed that procrastination is commonplace at JMSS. It was
found that Year 11s and 12s are more likely to procrastinate for longer,
however the difference between all three year levels is minimal.

Did the results meet your group's expectations?

The results did meet our expectations to an extent in terms of the average
length of time students procrastinate (for around 30 minutes in 2 hours), ways
students procrastinate (technology and social media), and how procrastination
affects academic results (From a little ranging to quite a lot) were all expected. However
it was expected that there would be a larger correlation between extra-curriculars,
and those doing accelerated study with increased procrastination. This did not end
up being the case as no correlation was found.

Anything interesting or unusual?

Procrastination was not directly related to what year level a participant was in
nor was it related to their extracurricular activities; what was interesting about
this was that no factor was correlated with whether a participant had experienced
procrastination. The most popular method of procrastination was watching YouTube, the
second being messaging friends and the third being social media; from this it can be
deduced that the internet and technology is a major cause of procrastination among JMSS
students, specifically social media as the top 3 methods of procrastination are all forms of social media.
