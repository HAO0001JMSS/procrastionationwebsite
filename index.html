Procrastination at JMSS

Procrastination is the act of delaying an important task… I'm going to finish writing this later…

No! That's procrastination! Putting off something important and choosing to do something more
enjoyable instead! In this case, not writing this introduction will cause me to miss the deadline
for this assignment!

Procrastination is a widespread problem, especially in high school, where students will leave any task, even
important ones assignments and study to the last minute, which can have a serious impact on grades and academic
performance, as found in data collected through surveying students ranging from year 10 to year 12 at JMSS.

As such, we designed this website to inform you on exactly how procrastination affects students, how students
begin and end their procrastination episodes, and how best to avoid procrastination in the future.

Why Procrastination?

Procrastination is a prominent issue for the student population at JMSS

Why this Topic?

This topic was chosen so students can better understand how prevalent the issue of procrastination is, and to propose potential solutions for readers having the problem, while also informing readers on the scientific findings regarding procrastination.

Purpose

This website has been created so students can better understand how to reduce procrastination in their lives. Data about procrastination which were collected from students in the school will also show readers that procrastination is an issue that many students experience, and that they aren't alone.

Audience

The intended audience are students between the ages of 15 to 18 studying in Victoria. We selected this age group as these students will begin or are currently enrolled into VCE classes, which can be very stressful, likely causing them to have a higher chance of feeling the effects of procrastination firsthand.
