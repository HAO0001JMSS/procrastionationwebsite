Our Proposal

What is your chosen infographic topic and why is this a topic you wish to research?

Procrastination has become a prominent issue for the student population at JMSS, as it can
heavily affect academic performance, to the point where students are unable to complete tasks
to the expected standard, causing problems in their future and career. This topic was chosen
so students can better understand how prevalent the issue is, and to propose potential solutions
for readers having the problem, while also informing the audience on the scientific findings regarding procrastination.

Purpose of the infographic website - Persuade, Inform or Entertain? What does it aim to achieve?

The infographic website will inform readers about what procrastination is and how it affects
academic performance and wellbeing. Accurate, scientific information about alleviating or
avoiding procrastination will be provided so students can better understand how to reduce
procrastination in their lives. Statistics about procrastination which were collected from
students in the school will also show readers that procrastination is an issue that many students
experience, and that they aren't alone.

Intended audience - Describe the type of students this infographic is targeted towards and why, eg age group, ethnicity, gender, interests, etc

The intended audience are students between the ages of 15 to 18 studying in Victoria. We
selected this age group as these students will begin or are currently enrolled into VCE
classes, which can be very stressful, likely causing them to have a higher chance of feeling
the effects of procrastination firsthand.

What information based on primary data are you aiming to collect and present on your infographic?

The information we plan to collect includes:

- An estimated amount of procrastination within students on a weekly basis
- Preferred forms of procrastination
- Extracurriculars and how much time is spent on a weekly basis on them
- If students believe that procrastination affects their academic performance
- Subject choices

Each of these questions will be separated by year level, as it is likely that the level of
procrastination differs between year levels. Overall, the collection of this data will enable
us to make an understanding of how the intended audience experiences procrastination, and what
characteristics of a student can contribute to procrastination.

What information based on secondary data are you aiming to research and present on your website?

The information our group will research that is based on secondary data is the science behind
procrastination and effective ways to prevent it, along with strategies to help students avoid procrastination in our lives.

What will you do to ensure the integrity of your primary and secondary data (i.e reliable and free from bias)?

In the case of primary data, we will ensure all work has been collected fairly with no bias.
This is done through having a high population of people that data is collected from. Further
questions will be asked to minimise possible risks of a faulty experiment, such as “any
extracurriculars outside of school”, as it may decrease the chances of procrastination, since
they may be more motivated to study. Another question will be, “what year level are you in this
year”, as some year levels may not have to put in as much effort to study as others.

Therefore each person will fall into two categories based on their year level and their extracurriculars. For each person there will be the question:
- How much work did you want to get done?
- How much did you get done?
- What was your way of procrastination?
- Have you tried to do anything to stop it?
- If so, what have you done?
- Has it worked?

With secondary data, we will ensure all outside data collected was from credible sources,
which will be done by checking the APA hyperlinks of websites and do external research if
many credited websites source similar information.

What will you do to ensure the ethical collection, storage and use of collected data?

Regarding the ethical processes behind the collection of the data, as a group, we will make
sure to not ask any personal questions or anything relating to the academic results of participants
that may cause discomfort. This will enable the data collection to remain neutral and unbiased.
Personal opinions will not interfere with the data collection and will be submitted purely based on
participant results. As an essential process, we will ask consent before the commencement of the data collection.

We will store all data on an usb to prevent it from being sent to outsiders, and set precautions such
as encrypted files for full protection against the data. Moreover, a google form will be sent out to
students. Those who complete the survey filled with our questions based on procrastination will give us
our primary data. This form will remain anonymous, and we will not know the name of the student to prevent
any leakage of data. The most obvious way to ensure full protection is to not share the data with anyone outside the group.
